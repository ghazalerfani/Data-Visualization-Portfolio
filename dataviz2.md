The first method of visualization only shows information for one year, not over time: although useful, it shows only part of the data. The second method of visualization is rather overwhelming: with 35 countries over about 20 years, there are many data points to look at. This visualization may be useful for detailed analysis, but can not be understood in under 30 seconds. 

The third method of visualization is a time-series Area Graph. Rather than showing all data points for all countries, the countries are grouped into continent. This allows the audience to gain a high level understanding of general government debt across the world very quickly. It also uses gradients of the color red (from Colorbrewer2.org) to highlight countries that have higher debt. If possible, I would prefer to have a horizontal line through the area of each continent that indicates 100%. That way, the audience could quickly see that Asia and North America have values over 100% whereas South America does not even come close to 100%. Most importantly, this third visualization is simple and easy to understand.
